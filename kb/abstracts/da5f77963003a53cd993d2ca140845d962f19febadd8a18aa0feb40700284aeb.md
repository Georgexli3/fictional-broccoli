MECO Engineering Company, Inc. submitted this Statement of Qualifications to the City of Macon, MO seeking selection as the professional engineering consultant for airfield development projects at Macon-Fower Memorial Airport. The SOQ presents MECO's 40-year history serving rural Missouri municipalities on aviation and multi-discipline infrastructure projects. Key service areas highlighted include airspace clearance zone expansion, FAA-compliant runway and taxiway design and rehabilitation, navigational lighting systems (PAPI, LED taxiway/runway lighting, UNICOM-triggered systems), Automated Weather Observation System (AWOS) installation, aviation fuel system design (100LL AVGAS and Jet A), airport master planning, drone-based surveying and topographic mapping, and federal funding navigation (FAA grant programs, Capital Improvement Plans). The SOQ also profiles MECO's broader engineering capabilities—civil site development, transportation, structural, MEP, heavy civil, and construction management—as context for the firm's depth of resources. The proposed project team includes Ryan Huseman, PE (Project Manager/MEP Engineer), Kevin W. Garnett, PE (Civil Engineer), James D. Bensman, PE, SE (Structural/Civil Engineer), and Andrew Long (Technical Manager). MECO noted its Kirksville, MO office is approximately 35 miles from Macon, enabling rapid on-site response.
